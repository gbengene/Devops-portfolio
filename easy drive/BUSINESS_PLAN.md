## Executive Summary

**Business Name:** Easy Drive (trade name under GB Trade and Logistics Inc.)
**Legal Entity:** GB Trade and Logistics Inc. (existing corporation)
**Location:** Greater Toronto Area (GTA), Ontario, Canada
**Model:** Peer-to-peer car rental marketplace — car owners list vehicles, gig drivers rent them, Easy Drive earns a platform commission
**Launch Target:** 10 listed vehicles within 60 days of launch
**Platform Fee:** 20% of each rental transaction (paid by renter)
**Founder:** GB Trade and Logistics Inc.

### The Problem — Two Sides

**For gig drivers:**
Uber Eats, DoorDash, and SkipTheDishes drivers in the GTA need reliable, commercially-insured vehicles but are locked out of traditional rentals due to hard credit checks, mileage caps, daily pricing that doesn't match weekly income cycles, and outright refusal to allow commercial delivery use.

**For car owners:**
Thousands of GTA residents own a second car, a spare vehicle, or a recently-upgraded vehicle sitting idle. They have no safe, structured channel to monetize it — especially not one that screens renters properly for commercial use and includes GPS and security requirements.

### The Solution

Easy Drive is a two-sided marketplace connecting:
- **Car owners (Hosts)** who want to earn passive income from underused vehicles
- **Gig drivers (Renters)** who need affordable, commercially-cleared, weekly vehicle access

Easy Drive earns a 20% platform fee on every rental. Neither side has to find the other — Easy Drive is the trusted intermediary, the verification layer, and the payment rail.

### Why This Model Wins

| Factor | Traditional Rental | Owning a Fleet | Easy Drive Marketplace |
|---|---|---|---|
| Startup capital | N/A | $23K–$37K | ~$5K–$9K |
| Vehicle liability | N/A | Full | None |
| Maintenance cost | N/A | $600–$800/mo/car | None |
| Scalability | Limited | Capital-constrained | Unlimited (asset-light) |
| Insurance risk | N/A | Carried by operator | Carried by owner |

## 1. Business Structure

### Legal Setup
- **Operating Entity:** GB Trade and Logistics Inc. (existing incorporation)
- **Trade Name:** Easy Drive — registered on Ontario Business Registry under GB Trade and Logistics Inc.
- **HST:** Existing CRA Business Number / HST registration applies — platform fee revenue reported under same legal entity
- **Model:** Pure marketplace platform — Easy Drive does not own, register, insure, or maintain any vehicles

### What Easy Drive Is and Is Not

**Easy Drive IS:**
- A technology platform connecting car owners and gig drivers
- A trust and verification layer (screening both hosts and renters)
- A payment processor (collecting rental fees and disbursing to hosts)
- A policy enforcer (mandatory GPS, kill switch, and insurance requirements for listed vehicles)

**Easy Drive IS NOT:**
- A vehicle owner or operator
- An insurance company or insurance intermediary
- A party to the rental agreement (the agreement is between host and renter)

This distinction is critical for liability, regulatory compliance, and capital efficiency.

## 2. How It Works

### For Car Owners (Hosts)

1. Apply to list a vehicle — submit vehicle info, photos, proof of insurance
2. Easy Drive reviews and approves the listing (condition, insurance, security hardware)
3. Host sets their weekly rate and availability calendar
4. When a gig driver books, host confirms or auto-accepts
5. Host earns 80% of the rental rate, paid weekly via Stripe direct deposit

### For Gig Drivers (Renters)

1. Apply on Easy Drive — submit licence, driver abstract, gig platform account screenshot
2. Easy Drive reviews and approves the renter profile (one-time)
3. Renter browses available vehicles by location, price, and type
4. Renter books a vehicle — pays weekly in advance
5. Renter picks up vehicle from host; drives for the week; returns

### Platform Fee Flow

```
Renter pays $350/week
├── Easy Drive platform fee (20%): $70
└── Host receives (80%): $280

At 10 cars rented at $350/week:
├── Gross rental volume: $3,500/week
├── Easy Drive revenue: $700/week → ~$3,000/month
└── Hosts earn: $2,800/week → ~$12,133/month combined
```

## 3. Market Analysis

### Two-Sided Market

**Supply side — Car Owners:**
- GTA has millions of registered vehicles; a meaningful % are underused second cars
- Target hosts: recent immigrants who upgraded vehicles, small operators with 2–5 cars, gig drivers who bought a newer car and want to monetize the old one
- Value proposition: $280–$360/week in passive income per vehicle (after platform fee)
- Annual earning potential per listed vehicle: $14,500–$18,700

**Demand side — Gig Drivers:**
- Uber Eats alone has 50,000+ active couriers in Ontario
- DoorDash, SkipTheDishes, Instacart add significant additional volume
- Even 0.1% of that market = 50 renters; at $350/week = $17,500/week in rental volume
- No dominant, legitimate, gig-focused rental marketplace exists in the GTA today

### Competitive Landscape

| Platform | Model | Credit Check | Gig-Cleared | Weekly Cost | GTA Supply |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Turo | P2P marketplace | Yes (credit) | No (personal use only) | $400–$600 | Strong |
| Enterprise / Budget | Traditional rental | Hard check | No | $500–$700+ | Strong |
| HyreCar (US) | Gig-specific marketplace | Soft check | Yes | $350–$500 | Weak in Canada |
| Facebook grey market | Informal | None | Uninsured | $200–$280 | Unstructured |
| **Easy Drive** | **Gig-specific marketplace** | **No hard check** | **Yes** | **$300–$450** | **Building** |

### Competitive Advantage

1. **Gig-first by design** — every listing is pre-cleared for commercial delivery use; Turo explicitly prohibits it
2. **No hard credit check** — removes the biggest access barrier for the target renter
3. **Security-first listings** — GPS and kill switch required on every vehicle; gives hosts confidence to list
4. **Weekly pricing model** — matches gig driver income cycles; daily pricing doesn't
5. **Community distribution** — GTA gig driver networks spread information fast; word of mouth is the acquisition channel

### Business Registrations

| Requirement | Details | Action |

| Municipal business license | Technology/marketplace platform | Apply to GTA municipality (~$150–$300) |
| HST collection | Platform fee revenue is HST-taxable | Use existing HST number; remit on platform fees only |

### What Easy Drive Does NOT Need (Model Change)

| Item | Why Not Required |
|---|---|
| RIN (vehicle registration) | Easy Drive does not own or register vehicles |
| Fleet insurance | Each host carries their own insurance |
| OMVIC license | No vehicle sales or dealer activities |
| CVOR | No commercial vehicle operation by Easy Drive |

### Marketplace Platform Legal Requirements

**Terms of Service:**
- Clearly defines Easy Drive as a platform, not a party to the rental
- Host and renter agree to a direct rental agreement with each other
- Easy Drive's role: verification, payment processing, dispute mediation
- Limitation of liability clauses protect the platform from vehicle-related claims

**Privacy (PIPEDA / Ontario):**
- Disclose GPS and kill switch requirements to renters before booking
- Collect only data needed for verification and payments
- Do not share personal data with third parties (including hosts) beyond what's necessary for the booking

**Consumer Protection Act, 2002:**
- All fees disclosed upfront before checkout
- Cancellation policy clearly stated
- Renter receives booking confirmation with all terms

**Platform Liability Shield:**
- Easy Drive mediates disputes but is not liable for vehicle damage, accidents, or renter behaviour
- Hosts are responsible for maintaining vehicle insurance and roadworthiness
- This must be explicit in the Terms of Service — engage a lawyer to draft

### Insurance — The Critical Policy Question

This is the most important compliance issue for the marketplace model.

**The problem:** Most personal auto policies in Ontario do not cover commercial delivery use (Uber Eats, DoorDash). If a host's personal policy doesn't cover delivery, and a renter has an accident while delivering, the claim may be denied.

**The solution — require hosts to carry a commercial-use endorsement:**
- Intact, Aviva, and Economical offer rideshare/delivery endorsements in Ontario
- Hosts must upload proof of a policy that explicitly covers commercial delivery use before listing
- Easy Drive reviews and approves the insurance certificate as part of listing approval
- Hosts without the endorsement cannot list — this is non-negotiable

**Platform Protection Plan (Phase 2):**
- Partner with a commercial insurance broker to offer hosts an optional top-up protection plan through Easy Drive
- This follows Turo's model — platform-negotiated group coverage as an add-on
- Do not launch this without legal and FSRA review; insurance distribution in Ontario requires RIBO licensing or a licensed broker partner
- Launch Phase 1 without this; add it once you have sufficient volume to negotiate

## 7. Renter Verification

No hard credit check — alternative verification model. Same rigour, different inputs.

### Renter Application (One-Time, Reusable Across Bookings)

| Check | What It Tells You | How to Get It |
|---|---|---|
| Government-issued photo ID | Identity | Upload via secure portal |
| Valid Ontario G or G2 licence | Legal right to drive | Verified in person or via MTO lookup |
| MTO driver abstract | Violations, suspensions, at-fault accidents | Renter pulls from MTO (~$12); uploaded to platform |
| Active gig platform account | They have income, they're verified by Uber/DoorDash | Screenshot of active account status |
| Security deposit ($500) | Financial skin in the game | Held by Stripe on booking confirmation |
| First week rent (upfront) | Commitment | Charged at booking |

### Red Flags — Automatic Rejection

- Suspended or revoked licence
- More than 2 at-fault accidents in 3 years
- No active gig platform account
- Unwilling to consent to GPS tracking and kill switch terms

### Renter Rating System

- After each rental, host rates renter (1–5 stars + notes)
- After each rental, renter rates host and vehicle (1–5 stars + notes)
- Renters below 3.5 stars are suspended pending review
- Hosts below 3.5 stars have listings paused pending review
- This two-sided trust layer is what separates Easy Drive from Facebook grey market

## 8. Security Requirements for Listed Vehicles

Every vehicle on Easy Drive must have:

| Component | Purpose | Hardware | Cost (borne by host) |
|---|---|---|---|
| GPS Tracker | Real-time location, trip history, speed alerts | Bouncie ($8/mo) | ~$50 device + $96/yr |
| Ignition Interrupt | Remote disable on payment failure or breach | PassTime or equivalent | $200–$350 installed |
| Geofencing | Alert if vehicle leaves Ontario | Configured in GPS platform | Included |

**Why require this on host-owned vehicles:**
- Hosts bear the risk of renting to a stranger — these tools are their protection
- Easy Drive can only enforce accountability if it has visibility into the vehicle
- Kill switch access (with audit log) is shared between host and Easy Drive admin
- Geofence alerts protect hosts from cross-provincial liability exposure

**Kill Switch Protocol:**
- Renter signs consent before first booking (platform-level, not per-rental)
- Either host or Easy Drive admin can activate via platform
- Every activation is logged: who triggered, timestamp, reason
- Ignition interrupt only — never cut power to a moving vehicle
- Renter receives written notice (SMS + email) when kill switch is activated

## 11. Operations

### Platform Workflows

**New Host Listing:**
1. Host submits application via portal
2. Easy Drive reviews: insurance cert (commercial use confirmed), photos, GPS/kill switch confirmation
3. Admin approves listing or requests corrections
4. Host sets rate + availability; listing goes live

**New Renter Booking:**
1. Approved renter browses available vehicles
2. Renter selects vehicle, chooses start date
3. Platform collects: deposit ($500) + first week rent + 20% platform fee
4. Host is notified; confirms (or auto-accepts)
5. Renter picks up vehicle from host; both parties complete inspection via the app (timestamped photos)
6. Platform releases host payout (80% of weekly rate) weekly

**Weekly Renewal:**
1. Stripe charges renter automatically each week
2. Renter and host get confirmation via SMS + email
3. If payment fails: renter gets 24-hour SMS/email notice; admin alerted; kill switch standby
4. If not resolved in 24 hours: breach protocol (GPS confirm location → kill switch → host and admin notified)

**Vehicle Return:**
1. Host inspects vehicle with renter present — photo documentation via app
2. Compare to intake checklist
3. Any damage noted → host submits claim within 48 hours → Easy Drive mediates
4. Clean return → deposit refunded to renter within 5 business days

### Dispute Resolution

Easy Drive operates as a neutral mediator:
- Both parties submit photos and notes via the platform
- Easy Drive admin reviews evidence and makes ruling
- Deposit is distributed according to ruling
- Escalated disputes (large damage claims) referred to host's insurance

## 15. Long-Term Vision

Easy Drive starts as a GTA-focused peer-to-peer car rental marketplace for gig drivers and scales to become Canada's dedicated gig-economy vehicle access platform.

**The asset-light advantage:**
Every new car that joins the platform is someone else's capital, someone else's insurance, someone else's maintenance. Easy Drive's job is to be the trust layer and the distribution channel. That is infinitely scalable in a way that fleet ownership is not.

**Network effects:**
- More hosts → more vehicle choice → more renters join
- More renters → faster bookings for hosts → more hosts list
- Better ratings → higher-quality community → lower damage rates → lower risk for everyone
